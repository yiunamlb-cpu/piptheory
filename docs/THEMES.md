## Regime Snapshot

**Stagflationary impulse from energy shock. Monetary policy globally reversing from "simultaneous ease" (2025) to "simultaneous hold or hike" (2026).** US growth still resilient, AI capex anchoring equity bid, but inflation re-accelerating due to Middle East oil shock. Dollar weakening *despite* hawkish drift elsewhere because rate differential is compressing — Fed is highest of the majors but the path is less hawkish than ECB/RBA's pivot. China still deflating, fourth year, fading as a global demand engine.

This is not a "Fed cutting, risk-on" regime. It's not a "hard recession" regime either. It's a stagflation-lite + AI capex tug-of-war.

## Active Themes

### 1. Energy-Driven Reflation — Conviction 8/10

**Thesis:** Iran/Middle East conflict has put a structural bid under oil. CPI jumped from 2.4% (Feb) to 3.3% (Mar), highest since May 2024, driven by gasoline (+18.9%) and fuel oil (+44.2%). Core inflation also picking up (2.6%). This is forcing central banks to reverse their easing bias.
**Supporting evidence:** ECB hike in June "all but inevitable" per Kazimir; RBA hiked third consecutive meeting; Fed shifted language from "remains somewhat elevated" to "is elevated"; 4 FOMC dissenters (most since 1992).
**Invalidation:** Sustained ceasefire reduces oil risk premium; CPI April print (May 12) shows reversal toward 2%.
**What it implies:** Long energy, long energy currencies (CAD, NOK, AUD), short bonds (long yields), structurally bearish for growth-sensitive duration plays.

### 2. ECB Hawkish Pivot — Conviction 7/10

**Thesis:** ECB is in catch-up mode. Energy shock + sticky services inflation forcing them to abandon the 2025 easing trajectory. Markets pricing 3 hikes in 2026, starting June. Deposit rate 2.0%, room to move.
**Supporting evidence:** Lagarde's April presser unanimous hold but with active hike debate; Bloomberg reporting Kazimir's "all but inevitable" June hike.
**Invalidation:** Eurozone PMI rolls over hard before June meeting; energy crisis resolves.
**What it implies:** Bullish EUR vs. USD on rate-differential compression. Bullish EUR vs. JPY (BoJ stuck). Watch for euro-strength to bleed into DAX weakness if export sensitivity matters.

### 3. Fed Stuck-at-Neutral, Skewed-to-Hike — Conviction 6/10

**Thesis:** Fed funds at 3.5–3.75% holding for the year. Market is pricing ~15% probability of a December hike, JPM expects no move until Q3 2027 hike. The shift from "next move is a cut" to "next move could be a hike" is the regime change. Even without an actual hike, the *re-pricing* of the path is what moves the dollar and yields.
**Supporting evidence:** April FOMC dissents, language shift, oil shock effect on near-term CPI.
**Invalidation:** Labor market crack (NFP miss + unemployment up); growth data rolls over; oil collapses.
**What it implies:** Bearish duration (long yields), but ambiguous for USD because path is *less* hawkish than ECB/RBA. The dollar is squeezed between safe-haven bid (Iran) and rate-differential decay.

### 4. China Deflationary Quagmire — Conviction 8/10

**Thesis:** Fourth consecutive year of deflation. PPI -2.6% YoY. Real estate -17.2%. Stimulus measures (1.3T yuan special bonds, 250B trade-in program) running but not changing the trajectory. China's lowest growth target on record (4.5-5%). This is a *durable* theme, not a tactical one.
**Supporting evidence:** PBOC continuing accommodative monetary policy, RRR cuts on the table, deflation broadening across sectors (EVs, e-commerce).
**Invalidation:** Decisive fiscal pivot toward consumer transfers (vs. supply-side); real estate stabilization.
**What it implies:** Bearish AUD on China-demand channel (note: this competes with AUD strength from RBA hikes — cross-currents, watch closely). Bearish industrial commodities ex-energy. Bearish CNY. Long gold (PBoC continued buying, store-of-value bid amid deflation).

### 5. AI Capex as Equity Anchor — Conviction 7/10

**Thesis:** AI buildout — datacenters, power, chips — is the single biggest US-specific tailwind keeping equities bid despite stagflation overhang. BlackRock and others framing it as the "anchor theme" of 2026. It's spreading beyond mega-cap tech into banks, utilities, healthcare.
**Supporting evidence:** Concentration risk flagged by every major asset manager; energy demand from datacenters reinforcing US energy story.
**Invalidation:** AI capex air pocket (one big company guides down on capex), or growth scare big enough to override the AI bid.
**What it implies:** Long ES/NQ on dips, but with real fragility — "high prices and complacent positioning" per BlackRock. Not a heroic-conviction long; a tactical pullback-buy theme. NQ probably stronger than ES (more direct AI exposure).
