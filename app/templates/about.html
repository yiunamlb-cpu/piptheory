About & Methodology

PIPTHEORY is a free, systematic Macro Currency Strength Meter. It scores each of the eight major currencies — USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, CHF, CAD, AUD and NZD — on a scale from +100 (very strong) to −100 (very weak), updated every day. Strength is always relative: a currency is only strong or weak compared to the others.

It's mechanical, not opinion

The score is calculated by a fixed, transparent formula from real economic and market data. There is no AI writing the numbers, no analyst's view, and nothing subjective. That means it's fully reproducible — the same inputs always produce the same score, so it never contradicts itself from one day to the next.

The five pillars

Each currency's score blends five fundamental forces, each measured against its own history and ranked across the eight majors:

  • Interest Rates — the short-term rate and 10-year bond yield. Currencies that pay more tend to attract global capital.
  • Growth — the labour market and the health of the underlying economy.
  • Positioning — how large institutional funds are positioned, from CFTC futures data.
  • Risk Mood — safe-haven currencies (USD, JPY, CHF) versus risk-on currencies (AUD, NZD, CAD), scaled by the current market mood.
  • Commodities — terms-of-trade tailwinds for commodity exporters.

These are combined into a single composite, ranked, and tracked over time. A currency pair's reading is simply the difference between the two currencies' scores (EUR/USD = EUR score − USD score).

Context signals

Two extra signals are shown for context but deliberately not folded into the score: a valuation read (whether a currency looks stretched versus its own recent range) and a price-vs-fundamental check (whether spot has already moved in line with the fundamentals, i.e. how much is "priced in"). We tested adding these to the score and they didn't improve it, so they stay as context only.

How to read it — honestly

Treat the meter as macro context, not a trading signal. A high score means the fundamental wind is at a currency's back; it does not predict tomorrow's price. Markets are forward-looking and often price the fundamental picture in advance, and no mechanical model sees political shocks or surprises. The score reflects the slow macro tide over weeks, not day-to-day noise.

Updates

The meter recomputes automatically every day. The "Updated" date is shown at the top of the homepage. It's daily macro context, not a real-time alert service.

What this is NOT

  • Not financial advice. Nothing here is a recommendation to buy, sell or hold anything.
  • Not a signal service. No entry levels, stops or targets.
  • Not personalised. Everyone sees the same numbers; it doesn't know your situation.
  • Not a crystal ball. The model can be wrong, and markets move on things it can't measure.
