A multi-agent macro research desk.

Every morning before US market open, a panel of specialist research agents reads the world's macro data, debates each instrument from multiple angles, and publishes a single daily brief. PIPTHEORY is that publication.

What this is

Macro research at the speed of the market — without a team of analysts.

Traditionally, this kind of cross-asset daily research is the product of a discretionary macro desk: half a dozen analysts, a strategist, a portfolio manager, and a contrarian or two. PIPTHEORY replicates that workflow as a panel of specialist AI agents — each focused on one piece of the puzzle, each producing structured analysis that the next agent reads and synthesises.

The result is the same kind of daily output a small institutional desk would publish: a regime read, an instrument-by-instrument directional view with confidence levels, the macro themes driving each call, and a single brief that ties it all together.

How it works

Five layers. Seven specialists.

Each daily run cascades through structured stages. Earlier stages read the world; later stages pressure-test the read.

  1. 01
    Specialists read the data

    Seven research panels run in parallel: a US Fed analyst, an ECB analyst, a Bank of England analyst, a Bank of Japan analyst, an inflation tracker, a market positioning analyst, and a geopolitical risk analyst. Each reads its own primary sources — official central-bank statements, government economic data, derivatives positioning, the news cycle — and produces a structured read.

  2. 02
    A senior strategist synthesises

    A cross-asset strategist reads all seven specialist outputs and the active macro themes, then produces a draft directional view for every instrument the desk covers — direction (long / short / no view), conviction (1-10), the driving themes behind the call, and the invalidation conditions.

  3. 03
    A contrarian attacks every view

    A dedicated red-team analyst, calibrated to be wrong 70% of the time on purpose, attacks the consensus on every instrument. Seven attack vectors: crowded positioning, narrative fragility, historical analog, cross-asset dissent, central-bank reaction, tail risk, news-shock reversal. The point isn't to be right — it's to make sure no draft view survives without being properly stress-tested.

  4. 04
    A three-judge council debates

    For each instrument where the strategist's conviction is 5+, a Bull analyst makes the strongest long case, a Bear analyst makes the strongest short case, and a Judge weighs both with explicit probability decomposition before assigning the final direction + conviction. This runs per-instrument and in parallel — by the end of the morning every covered symbol has been argued from both sides and adjudicated.

  5. 05
    The daily brief is written

    A PM-style writer takes every adjudicated view and produces the day's brief in plain English — the regime read, the highest-conviction calls, the cross-asset narrative tying them together. That's what you read at the top of the homepage.

How often it updates

Daily, before US market open.

A fresh publication appears every weekday at approximately 07:00 UTC (02:00 ET / 08:00 CET). On weekends the previous Friday's publication stays at the top until Monday's sync. The exact "last sync" time is shown at the top of every page.

When a major event happens mid-day — a central-bank decision, a surprise data print, a geopolitical shock — it's reflected in the next morning's publication rather than mid-day. PIPTHEORY is daily research, not a real-time alert service.

What's covered

Nine instruments. Five regime themes.

The instrument universe is deliberately small and deliberately liquid: the US Dollar Index (DXY), four major FX pairs (EURUSD, USDJPY, GBPUSD, AUDUSD), two US equity indices (S&P 500 futures ES, Nasdaq 100 futures NQ), and two commodity benchmarks (Gold GC, WTI Crude CL). These nine instruments together cover the FX, equity, and commodity expressions of every major macro theme.

The theme set evolves with the regime — typically 4 to 6 active themes at any time, each with its own conviction rating. The live theme list is on the homepage under "Macro picture."

Important

What this is NOT.

  • Not financial advice. Nothing on PIPTHEORY is a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any financial instrument.
  • Not a signal service. There are no entry levels, no stops, no targets, no execution guidance of any kind. PIPTHEORY publishes directional views and reasoning, not trades.
  • Not a substitute for your own research. The agents read public data and form views. They can be wrong. Markets move on things the panel doesn't see. Use this to inform your thinking, not to delegate it.
  • Not personalised. The same publication is delivered to every reader. It does not know your risk tolerance, account size, time horizon, or jurisdiction.

Markets carry the risk of loss. Past performance is not indicative of future results. Consult a licensed financial adviser in your jurisdiction before acting on any view discussed on this site.
