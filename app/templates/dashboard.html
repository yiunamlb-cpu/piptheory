    How to read this page
    

      
Every morning the system looks at all {{ active_universe|length }}
      instruments you can trade on FTMO ({{ active_universe|join(', ') }}) and
      gives each one a view. The page above sorts them into three groups so
      you don't have to read every card.


      
How instruments get sorted into the three groups:

      
    
        
  • Tradable now Macro view is
          strong AND the chart is at a clean spot. The system is saying "if
          you're going to take this kind of trade, today's a good day for
          it." Top section.
    • 
        
  • Watch Macro view is strong
          but the chart isn't ready yet (price extended, or near a level, or
          there's a big news event in 2-3 days). Wait for a cleaner spot.
          Middle section.
    • 
        
  • Skip Macro view holds but
          the chart is fighting it (extended late-chase, broken trend, etc.).
          The macro lean isn't enough on its own — pass on this one and look
          again tomorrow. Bottom section, collapsed.
    • 
        
  • Low conviction Macro view
          isn't strong enough (under 5/10) to act on at all. Bottom section,
          collapsed.
    • 
      


      
The conviction number (e.g. 6/10). Think of it as
      strength of the macro view, not a probability. 8+ is rare and strong;
      6-7 is solid; 5 is "exists but weak"; under 5 is noise.
      Importantly: a 6/10 today and a 5/10 tomorrow doesn't mean
      anything changed — the AI gives slightly different answers each
      run. Only worry when the number drops 2+ points or the direction flips
      (long → short).


      
The Brief tab below is the system's actual written
      summary of the day. The Reasoning tab shows everything
      that went into producing each card if you want to dig in.

Active positions

        

          Trades you've opened on FTMO. The system never executes — you record
          entries here so the system can flag when the macro thesis or chart
          structure has materially changed. Close marks a trade exit; closed
          trades go to the journal for monthly review.
        

      


      
      

        How to read this tab
        

          
Once you've taken a trade and recorded it here, you'll see a card
            for it. Each card has a coloured pill at the top-right that tells you
            what the system thinks you should do.


          
The six pills, simplest to most urgent:


          
    
            
  • 
              Hold
              
    
                All good. Don't touch the trade. Most days you'll see this.
              
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Review
              
    
                Heads-up only. The trade is still on track, but the chart isn't
                as clean as when you entered. You don't need to do
                anything. Just glance at it. Worth thinking about only
                if this stays for several days and you're losing money.
              
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Trail stop
              
    
                You're nicely in profit and the view still holds. Move your stop
                up so a normal pullback won't take away all your gains. Don't
                tighten too much — leave room for normal market noise.
              
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Trim
              
    
                Take some money off the table. The trade is still alive, but the
                case for it has weakened (the system used to be 7/10 confident,
                now it's 4/10, for example). Sell roughly half; let the rest run
                in case it firms back up.
              
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Close
              
    
                The system has changed its mind. Either it now thinks the market
                will move against your trade, or the chart has turned
                ugly. Get out. If you still believe in your view, wait for a
                cleaner setup and re-enter then.
              
    
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Emergency close
              
    
                Your stop level has been hit. Close the trade right
                now. Don't argue. That's exactly what the stop was for —
                you don't get to vote on it once price is there.
              
    
            
    • 
          


          
Day-to-day wobble — what to ignore. The
            conviction number (e.g. 6/10) wobbles by a point most days. That's
            normal — the AI gives slightly different answers each run even on
            the same data. A 6 today, 5 tomorrow, 6 the day after means nothing
            changed. Only worry when the number drops a lot (3+ points from where
            you opened) or the bias flips (your long becomes the system's short).
            The pills already account for this — if it's just noise, you'll see
            Hold or Review, not Close.


          
What the buttons on each card do:

          
    
            
  • Mark closed — Use this when you've already
              closed the trade on FTMO. Type the price you exited at and one
              line about why.
    • 
            
  • Add note — Add a dated note to the trade.
              Useful for things like "trimmed half here", "moved stop up",
              "ignored the system and held — was right". Builds up the story
              of the trade for your monthly review.
    • 
            
  • Move stop / Set stop — Update your stop level.
              The system never moves it for you; you always type the number.
    • 
            
  • Delete — Only use if you opened the position
              by mistake. If you actually closed the trade, use "Mark closed"
              instead so it goes into your trade journal.
    •
