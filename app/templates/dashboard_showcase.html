◆ Workflow / how today's call was produced

Five layers, seven specialists.

Each pipeline run cascades through structured layers. Earlier layers are about reading the world; later layers about pressure-testing the read.

01
Specialists read the data FRED · COT · central banks · news log
Seven Layer 1 agents fire in parallel on every run: Inflation Tracker, Positioning Analyst, Fed-Watcher (with FOMC text), ECB-Watcher (with Lagarde transcript), BoE-Watcher (with MPC minutes), BoJ-Watcher (PDF-extracted Policy Board statement), Geopolitical Risk. Each writes structured output the Strategist can synthesise.
02
Strategist synthesises per-instrument bias cards
A senior FX & cross-asset strategist persona reads all Layer 1 output plus your THEMES.md regime tracker plus the recent_events.yaml news log. Produces one bias card per instrument: direction, conviction, driving themes, specialist support, invalidation, cross-asset confirmation.
03
Contrarian red-teams attack vector hierarchy
A persistent contrarian persona attacks the consensus on every instrument. Seven attack vectors: crowded positioning, narrative fragility, historical analog, cross-asset dissent, central-bank reaction, tail risk, news-shock reversal (announcement-driven regimes). Calibrated to be wrong 70% of the time on purpose.
04
Council debates bull → bear → judge per instrument
For each instrument the Strategist scored ≥ 5/10: a Bull advocate makes the strongest long case; a Bear makes the strongest short case; a Judge weighs them with explicit probability decomposition and assigns final direction + conviction. Runs in parallel across instruments.
05
Tradability Filter gates the chart timeframe-aware structural review
A separate setup-reviewer persona checks whether price right now is at a clean structural location consistent with the macro bias. Different rule sets per timeframe (tactical / swing / positional). Verdict: tradable_now / watch / pass_despite_bias / below_threshold.
