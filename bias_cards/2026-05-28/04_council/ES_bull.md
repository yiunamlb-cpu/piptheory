PRIMARY DRIVER:
  Theme: Theme 5 — AI Capex as Equity Anchor (conviction 7/10)
  Specialist support: Fed-Watcher (dovish hold, easing bias → lower discount rate supports equities); Inflation Tracker (neutral — not flagging an imminent inflation shock that would force a hawkish reprice)
  Mechanism: AI infrastructure buildout — datacenters, power, chips — is the dominant US equity narrative. It is spreading beyond mega-cap tech into banks, utilities, and healthcare, broadening the bid. The Fed's dovish hold keeps the discount rate anchored, which directly supports the present value of these long-duration growth assets. As long as the capex cycle is intact, the equity market has a structural bid that cyclical headwinds (oil, inflation) can dent but not break.

SUPPORTING EVIDENCE:
  1. BlackRock and other major asset managers explicitly frame AI capex as the "anchor theme" of 2026 — this is not a fringe view, it is the institutional consensus driving capital allocation.
  2. The Fed's April hold with an easing bias (Fed-Watcher output) removes the rate-hike tail risk that would otherwise compress multiples. The market is pricing ~15% probability of a December hike — that means an 85% probability of *no hike*, which is the base case supporting equities.
  3. The AI bid is broadening beyond tech — banks, utilities, healthcare are all capex beneficiaries. This diversification makes the theme more resilient than a narrow mega-cap tech rally.

CATALYST:
  Specific event: A major AI-related earnings beat or capex *increase* guidance from a bellwether (NVDA, MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL) during the next earnings cycle, or a softer-than-expected May 12 CPI print that reduces the stagflation overhang and lets the AI bid run clean.
  Probability: medium
  Timing window: May 12 CPI through Q2 earnings pre-announcements (late May–early June)

TIMEFRAME: swing (weeks)

INVALIDATION: A specific, named major tech firm (NVDA, MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL) guiding down AI-related capex spending. A CPI print that accelerates further (above 3.5% headline) and forces Fed speakers to explicitly open the door to a June hike. An ISM Manufacturing print below 45 that signals a growth scare severe enough to overwhelm the structural AI bid.

CONTRARIAN ENGAGEMENT:
  Strongest contrarian point against this bull case: The AI capex trade is heavily concentrated and consensus; a single guidance cut from a major tech firm could trigger a 5–10% correction. No positioning data is available to assess crowding.
  Rebuttal: The contrarian's point is valid and is the *primary risk* to the bull case — but it is a *conditional* risk, not a *current* fact. The bull case does not require the absence of this risk; it requires that the catalyst has not yet fired. Until a named firm actually guides down capex, the structural bid remains intact. The absence of positioning data cuts both ways — we cannot *assume* crowding. Moreover, consensus is not inherently bearish; the AI capex theme has been consensus for 18+ months and equities have rallied through it. Consensus becomes dangerous when it is *fully priced and levered* — we have no evidence of that yet. The bull case survives by acknowledging the fragility and watching for the specific invalidation trigger, not by pre-emptively folding to it.

CONFIDENCE IN THIS BULL CASE: 6/10
