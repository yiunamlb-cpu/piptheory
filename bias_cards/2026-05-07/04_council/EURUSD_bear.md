  Rebuttal: This narrative under‑appreciates the ECB’s sensitivity to headline inflation — which has historically dominated their reaction function. If oil retreats, headline HICP will quickly decelerate, taking the “active hike debate” off the table. The Governing Council will not hike into a fading inflation impulse just for credibility; they will cite external disinflationary forces and revert to data‑dependence. On the Fed side, a CPI print above 3.5% in an election year would attract intense political attention; the four dissenters at the April meeting already showed a hawkish impulse that needs only one more high‑inflation data point to convert into a December hike — a scenario the market is clearly *not* pricing. The bull case works only if *both* the ECB ignores energy disinflation *and* the Fed ignores a re‑acceleration in US prices. That is a fragile conjunction.

CONFIDENCE IN THIS BEAR CASE: 5/10  
  The argument is logically coherent and identifies a genuine vulnerability with a clear catalyst. However, it runs against the dominant theme‑cluster (ECB hawkish, neutral Fed) and relies on a tail‑event or a CPI surprise that, while plausible, is not the base case. This is a fat‑tail short thesis, not a high‑conviction trend play.
