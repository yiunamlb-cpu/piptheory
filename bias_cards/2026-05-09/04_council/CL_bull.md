CONTRARIAN ENGAGEMENT:  
  Strongest contrarian point: the oil market is trading a binary “conflict‑or‑ceasefire” headline; a sudden diplomatic breakthrough can cause a $5–$10 overnight gap down, reversing the entire premium within days. Historical analogs (1990 Gulf War, 2019 Aramco) show rapid collapse once a resolution surfaces.  
  Rebuttal: The 35% probability assigned to that tail event is acknowledged, but it leaves 65% weight on the base case of continued conflict. Base case probabilities drive positioning. The bull case does not ignore the binary risk — it *brackets* it with a near‑term stop and a size cap. Furthermore, the historical analogs cited were resolved quickly only after unambiguous military victories or diplomatic settlements; current Iran conflict shows no credible pathway to de‑escalation yet, making the “announcement‑driven surprise” risk no higher than any other geopolitical trade. Should a ceasefire occur, the initial gap is painful but survivable if risk is calibrated; afterward, the market will re‑price to underlying physical supply/demand, which is still tight given years of underinvestment and resilient demand, limiting the ultimate retracement. The bull case remains compelling for a swing window because the asymmetry tilts toward upside from a non‑crowded base, while the downside is a defined, low‑probability shock that can be managed with discipline.

CONFIDENCE IN THIS BULL CASE: 7/10  
  Note: This is the *strength of the case*, not P(bull wins). The Judge does the probability weighting. The 7/10 reflects robust thematic alignment, non‑crowded positioning, and a clear catalyst path; the principal deduction is the binary ceasefire tail, which is already bracketed and does not negate the structural argument.
