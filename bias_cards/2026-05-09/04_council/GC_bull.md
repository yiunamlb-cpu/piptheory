Thus, a hot CPI forces a hawkish *hold*, not a surprise 50bp hike — breakevens run, nominal yields lag, real yields *stay compressed or fall*. This asymmetry means CPI is a bullish catalyst, not a bearish one. On central bank buying: PBoC buying has continued through gold corrections before; it’s a reserve diversification strategy, not a speculative flow — it provides a hard floor, not a fair‑weather friend. The ceasefire tail risk is real but bracketed: a ceasefire alone cannot reverse energy reflation in the next 4 weeks; sanctions and supply‑chain damage will keep oil bid and inflation elevated. The bull case only requires that a ceasefire *does not occur* before the CPI‑catalyst window; a 75% probability suffices.
