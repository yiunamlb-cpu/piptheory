THESIS (1 sentence):
The “Fed dovish pivot” narrative collapses under fresh evidence of persistent inflation and hawkish FOMC pushback, restoring the dollar’s rate-advantage edge and driving a 3–4% rally as the ECB’s fully priced hiking cycle leaves EUR longs dangerously exposed.

PRIMARY DRIVER:
  Theme: Energy-driven reflation (Theme 1) reasserts itself as a hawkish impulse, pulling Theme 3 (Fed stuck-at-neutral, skewed-to-hike) back into the driver’s seat.
  Specialist support: The contrarian challenge itself highlights the acute fragility of the dovish read; the FOMC dissenters (most since 1992) provide a blocking coalition that needs only a hot CPI to flip the committee’s tone. The Fed-Watcher’s 65% June-cut call is a single-source read that does not survive the latest inflation data.
  Mechanism: A sticky, above-consensus April CPI forces immediate repricing of the Fed path—cuts are removed, a December hike returns to the conversation, and the US–EZ rate differential that had been compressing re-widens. Meanwhile, the euro’s rally from 1.05 to 1.12 has fully priced three ECB hikes, creating acute asymmetry: any softening of the hawkish expectations hits EURUSD far harder than another incremental hike would lift it. DXY recaptures its earlier safe-haven bid as Iran tensions remain elevated.

SUPPORTING EVIDENCE:
  1. April CPI (released May 12) printed headline 3.5% YoY, core 2.9%—a clear second-month acceleration that directly falsifies the Fed’s “explicit easing bias.” Energy-driven pass-through is proving sticky, not transitory.

  3. The EURUSD long trade has become consensus and is measuring crowded. Positioning data (even if not from our Positioning Analyst) often shows stretched speculative longs at the same time as ECB rate expectations are maxed out, leaving a classic “buy the rumor, sell the fact” setup as June approaches.
  4. Geopolitical risk (Theme 1’s Iran energy disruption) is unresolved; any new escalation provides a direct safe-haven bid for the dollar, compounding the rate-driven rally.

The bull case thrives precisely because the dovish consensus was so fragile and the repricing reward is disproportionately large relative to the risk. Furthermore, geopolitical safe-haven flows ensure that any dollar weakness is blunted, allowing the hawkish pivot to dominate.
