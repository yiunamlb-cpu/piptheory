CONTRARIAN ENGAGEMENT:  
Strongest contrarian point against this bull case: *The 9/10 consensus is a unified bullish tower requiring all pillars to hold; a hot CPI would spike real yields and the dollar, snapping two legs. Once the short‑covering exhausts, gold could suffer a sharp “sell the fact” drawdown, as it historically does at sentiment peaks.*  
Rebuttal: The CPI pillar has already been tested and held — the April print did not produce a yield spike or hawkish repricing; real yields are lower, not higher, post‑CPI. The consensus was validated, not weakened.

If one pillar softens (e.g., geopolitical premium fades), the other two can carry the trend, as in 2019‑2020 when trade‑war fears ebbed but real yields collapsed.  
On sentiment peaks: this is not a sentiment extreme. COT net longs are in the 14th percentile — that is a positioning *trough*, not a peak. The “sell the fact” risk is low when the market is still under‑positioned. Historically, gold’s violent corrections come from overcrowded long positioning (e.g., 2020 post‑vaccine), not from short‑squeeze setups.  

CONFIDENCE IN THIS BULL CASE: 9/10  
Note: strength of the case, not P(bull wins). The alignment of real yields, positioning, central bank buying, and a cleared CPI hurdle is as strong as gold setups get.
