CONTRARIAN ENGAGEMENT:

Rebuttal: That narrative conflates two different regimes. In 2008/2011 the ECB was hiking into a sovereign‑bank crisis with a currency union on the brink; today’s banking system is resilient, and core services inflation (2.6%) has its own momentum beyond energy. An oil pullback alone won’t unwind the ECB determination, especially while the Iran situation remains unresolved and energy supply risks persist. Even if a single soft CPI print introduces doubt, the baseline remains an ECB that trails inflation and must act – the market’s hawkish pricing won’t vanish at one data point. The bull case can survive a modest slowdown; it only breaks if the ECB explicitly steps back from its tightening path, not if the timing shifts by a meeting.

The rate‑differential thrust and clean positioning profile make it a strong, layered argument that fades only under a full‑blown ECB capitulation or a disorderly risk‑off dollar surge that overrides rate dynamics.

The trade works as long as the ECB keeps its nerve and core inflation doesn’t implode; the biggest risk is a premature ceasefire, but even then the euro’s undervaluation and the ECB’s belated resolve provide a floor.
