KEY OBSERVATIONS:
  • **Risk assessment shifted decisively:** The statement removed the gentle “tilted” language and now states that upside inflation risks and downside growth risks have *intensified*. Risk to inflation is firmly to the upside, to growth to the downside – a stark departure from the March meeting.
  • **Hike openly debated, hold was tactical:** Lagarde revealed that the Council debated a rate hike “at length and in depth”. The unanimous decision to hold is not a dovish signal; it reflects a deliberate choice to gather more data on second‑round effects and energy price persistence over the next six weeks. This is a “waiting to hike” stance.
  • **No stagflation narrative from the ECB:** Lagarde forcefully pushed back against “stagflation” characterisations, noting growth projections (0.9%, 1.3%, 1.4% for successive years) and a totally different institutional framework. The ECB sees a negative supply shock, not a 1970s‑style spiral, and is determined to tame inflation.
  • **Critical focus on second‑round effects:** The Council is watching wages, selling price expectations, and inflation expectations. Lagarde noted *no* second‑round effects yet, only direct/indirect ones. This data‑gap is the reason for the hold; if second‑round effects emerge by June, a hike is virtually certain.

The smooth functioning of monetary policy transmission is affirmed, suggesting peripheral spreads (like BTP‑Bund) are not yet alarming enough to limit the hawkish direction. However, this could change if energy‑price fears trigger a flight to quality.
