The last comparable short‑USD squeeze (2021‑22) took EURUSD from 1.15 to 1.05 in a few weeks.

If the meeting merely delivers a 25bp hike without aggressive forward guidance, the EUR will sell the fact, and a simultaneous hawkish Fed repricing will compound the move.

BULL ENGAGEMENT:  
Strongest bull point against this bear case: The ECB’s independent hiking cycle is just starting, with rate‑differential compression driving a multi‑month trend toward 1.14, and EURUSD positioning is not crowded, so any USD squeeze would be a dip to buy.

A 3.95% CPI reading is a game‑changer that can reprice Fed expectations within a single week, erasing the compression advantage. Furthermore, “EUR positioning is clean” does not insulate the pair from a broad dollar bid – in a liquidation, correlations go to 1, and EURUSD will fall alongside AUDUSD. The “dip to buy” logic holds only if the squeeze is small and the Fed remains dovish. This bear case specifically targets the scenario where the squeeze is severe enough to break below 1.06, at which point the technical picture shifts from “pullback” to “potential trend reversal.” The bull case asks the judge to trust that crowded short‑dollar positions will unwind softly – history suggests the opposite.  

CONFIDENCE IN THIS BEAR CASE: 5/10  
Note: The bear case is a tactical, event‑driven scenario, not a high‑conviction structural view. It relies on a specific trigger (Fed hawkish shift) and crowded positioning, which together create asymmetric downside risk. The bias in the regime is still EURUSD bullish, but the setup for a sharp contra‑move is unusually rich.
