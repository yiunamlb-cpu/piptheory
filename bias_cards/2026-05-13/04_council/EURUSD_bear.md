Conversely, a *hot* CPI would strengthen the dollar if it pushes the Fed toward a hike – so the bull’s “asymmetry” actually cuts both ways, and the positioning danger is acute on the long side right now. The bear case brackets the bull’s claim: it requires that the CPI print is either soft enough to gut ECB expectations or hot enough to revive Fed hawkishness; both scenarios are negative for EURUSD. Only a “Goldilocks” print that keeps reflation alive without spooking the Fed supports the bull case – and that’s a narrow window.
