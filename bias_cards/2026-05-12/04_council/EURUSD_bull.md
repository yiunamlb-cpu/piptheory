THESIS (1 sentence):  
The ECB’s hiking cycle has only just begun, while the Fed is trapped in neutral, creating a multi‑month rate‑differential compression that will drive EURUSD above 1.12; pullbacks to 1.08–1.09 are high‑conviction buying opportunities.

PRIMARY DRIVER:  
Theme: Theme 2 (ECB Hawkish Pivot) — conviction 7/10, with a June hike “all but inevitable” and further hikes likely as energy‑driven inflation persists.  
Specialist support: ECB‑Watcher strongly supports (P(hike) ~75%, explicit debate); Fed‑Watcher supports (dovish hold, easing bias, market underpricing cuts); Positioning Analyst neutral (no headwind).  
Mechanism: As the ECB hikes while the Fed holds, the 2‑year yield spread (EUR–US) compresses, directly lifting EURUSD. The ECB’s catch‑up mode is structural, not a one‑off, because the energy shock is persistent and services inflation sticky.

1. ECB’s April statement and Lagarde’s presser confirmed an active hike debate; Kazimir called the June hike “all but inevitable” (Bloomberg, May 4). Markets price three hikes in 2026, starting June.

Probability: Soft CPI probability ~40% (market consensus uncertain), but even a neutral/hot CPI does not derail the ECB hiking path; it only delays the move.  
Timing window: Immediate (CPI day) through June ECB meeting.

TIMEFRAME: swing (weeks) → positional (months)

CONTRARIAN ENGAGEMENT:  
Strongest contrarian point: The market has fully priced the June hike, making the trade fragile; a hot US CPI today could revive the “Fed skewed‑to‑hike” narrative, widening spreads and causing a 1.5–2 figure drop.  
Rebuttal: The contrarian overestimates the Fed’s reaction function. Even a hot CPI (core >0.4%) would not immediately shift the Fed to a hiking bias; the bar for a hike is extremely high given the committee’s dovish lean and the lagged effects of prior tightening. The Fed is more likely to extend the hold, not hike. Meanwhile, the ECB’s hiking cycle is driven by domestic energy‑driven inflation that is not sensitive to US data. A temporary dollar spike on a hot CPI would be faded as the ECB’s June hike approaches, providing an even better entry. The structural rate‑differential compression remains intact. The contrarian’s 30% probability of a 1.5–2 figure drop is plausible for a tactical whipsaw, but the bull case is for a swing/positional timeframe where such dips are buying opportunities. The bull case brackets the event risk: if CPI is hot, we buy the dip at 1.07; if CPI is soft, we ride the breakout.

CONFIDENCE IN THIS BULL CASE: 7/10  
Note: The structural thesis is robust, but today’s CPI introduces tactical uncertainty. The case strength is high because the ECB path is clear and the Fed is stuck, but we acknowledge the near‑term event risk. The Judge should weigh this as a strong bull case with a defined entry zone and invalidation.
