**KEY OBSERVATIONS:**

1. **Council debated a hike but held unanimously** – Lagarde revealed that the Governing Council “debated, at length and in depth, a decision to possibly hike” but ultimately decided unanimously to keep rates unchanged. This places the ECB in an active debate, not a passive wait; a hike remains the baseline unless data deteriorates sharply.
2. **Language hardened on risks** – The statement now says “risks to the inflation outlook are to the upside” and “risks to growth are to the downside,” dropping the previous “tilted” qualifier. This is a meaningful hawkish drift, signalling that the risk management calculus now points toward further tightening.
3. **No second‑round effects yet, but June is the crucial checkpoint** – Lagarde stressed that the ECB sees “direct effects,” some “indirect effects,” but no material second‑round effects (wage spirals, de‑anchoring) so far. The decision to wait six weeks is specifically to gather hard data on wages, selling prices, commodity pass‑through, and corporate telephone survey results to determine whether second‑round effects are materialising.
4. **Supply‑shock framework, not stagflation** – Lagarde explicitly rejects the “stagflation” label (park it in the 1970s). The ECB’s reaction function, as outlined in the 2025 strategy review, treats a large negative supply shock with a “forceful or persistent” reaction. The Council is acutely aware of the 2011 mistake (hiking too early) but equally wary of being “a little bit too late,” framing the June decision as a delicate balance.
5. **Periphery spread risk not yet flagged, but TPI is implicit** – The statement makes no mention of sovereign spread fragmentation, and the press conference did not highlight BTP‑Bund spreads. However, the unified language and the Vice‑President’s strong assertion that banks are resilient suggest the TPI backstop is still seen as credible. A sudden widening of Italian spreads could act as a constraint on the pace of hiking, but for now this channel is dormant.

**GAP vs MARKET:**

**Aligning hawkishly.** Market pricing had already embedded a June hike, and Lagarde’s remarks did nothing to push back – in fact, she said “directionally, I know where we’re heading.” The risk is that the ECB may be forced to deliver more than the two hikes currently priced if energy‑price pressures prove persistent and second‑round effects emerge. Consequently, EUR should remain supported on dips, Bund yields (particularly the front end) face upward pressure, and peripheral spreads could grind wider if growth fears intensify, though they are not the dominant driver yet.

**NEXT CATALYST:**  
**ECB Governing Council meeting, 9‑10 June 2026** – updated staff macroeconomic projections, new sensitivity scenarios, and a live hike decision. Before then, any speeches by Schnabel, Lane, or hawkish national governors will be critical to gauge how the debate is evolving.

**INVALIDATION:**  
A rapid resolution of the Middle East conflict that sends energy prices sharply lower, combined with data showing no second‑round effects (sub‑3% core CPI, wage growth easing) and PMIs pointing to a serious downturn. In that scenario, the June hike could be postponed or even abandoned, shifting the stance back toward a neutral hold.
